The Other Encounter

A science performance and research by Futurice













Have you ever wondered what the world looks like through the eyes of a robot? If you were a robot, what would you say to a human? As a human, what would you say to a robot in an intimate conversation?

In The Other Encounter - a science performance by Futurice, we study human - robot interactions. We explore the concept of trust, the future of communication, and the future self. What is means to be a human in the age of emerging tech and how can technology enhance our lives. What are the requirements for social robots in urban environments? Most of all, we are studying, what are the problems, that social robots can help solving in our present and future society.

The first ‘Encounter’ happened on Tuesday September 11th at Helsinki Design Week. The audience was able to try out what it’s like to have a discussion with a robot, and they followed a discussion between a robot and a human. 18 Participants signed up as interviewers, who had a chance to ask Momo anything from politics to meaning of life. Three brave participants: media person Tuomas Enbuske, musician Kasmir and philosopher Johanna Ahola-Launonen gave their voices to Momo and took the challenge to answer these tough and yet unknown questions. Audience witnessed the wide range of discussion from getting married to a toaster - to building a world wide peace.

Momo met its guests in the window of the Columbia Road - a digital growth consultancy by Futurice. The Helsinki Design Week audience could walk by, watch the discussion and listen to the conversations from the speakers outside. The first Other Encounter -event was a co-operation between Futurice, Futurice Robotics, Columbia Road and Apple Cyber Collective.

Meet Momo

Momo the robot is an InMoov, designed by Gael Langevin and built by Futurice, Digitalents, Aalto university and Metropolia University of Applied Sciences. As a part of the HDW18 event, momo was wearing a unique dress by Jatuli.

The Research

The Other Encounter is part of a bigger research project, where Futurice is creating an open knowledge about how social robots should look and feel, what kind of tasks they should perform and how should the human - robot interaction happen and in what kind of situations. The research combines art, science and technology, this gives us experimental tools to dive deeper into a core of what it means to be human.
